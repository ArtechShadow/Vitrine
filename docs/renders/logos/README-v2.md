# Vitrine — Logo Exploration, SHEET v2 (2026-07-02)

A **second** eight-concept identity study for **Vitrine**, the capture-adaptive pipeline that
turns ordinary handheld video/photos of a real room into a structured, textured, game-ready
3D scene (Gaussian splatting → meshes) for Unreal Engine 5.8 / Blender. *Vitrine* is French for
a **glass display case / showcase** — a museum vitrine for reconstructed worlds.

Where **[v1](README.md)** was cool, thin-line, glassy and archival-elegant (warm museum paper,
precious restraint), **v2 is its bolder sibling**: warm, graphic, **risograph-editorial**, and
full of motion. It treats reconstruction as a **physical, ink-stained act** — order pulled
violently out of noise — rather than a serene glass cabinet. Nothing here repeats a v1 mark.

- **Sheet:** [`vitrine-logo-sheet-v2-2026-07-02.png`](vitrine-logo-sheet-v2-2026-07-02.png) — 3840×3276, single designed exploration board.
- **Marks:** [`marks-v2/`](marks-v2/) — the eight individual lockups (2048px PNG each).
- **How it was made:** ideation via a genuine multi-turn **dialogue with ZAI GLM-5.2** (see below);
  marks generated with Google **Gemini 3 Pro Image ("Nano Banana Pro", `gemini-3-pro-image`)** via
  `report/poster/nano_banana.py` (native 2K, `1:1`); board composed with Pillow (DejaVu + Liberation type).

## The v2 stance (vs v1)

v1 answered "what is Vitrine?" with *glass, precision, curation* — cool cyan/violet/brass on museum
paper, hairline geometry, a cabinet you admire quietly. v2 answers the **same brief from the opposite
temperament**: it foregrounds the *verb* of the product — **capture → diagnose → route → resolve** —
and renders that as poster-grade, hand-inked, mid-transformation energy.

Everything reads as one family through a shared **warm risograph system**:

- **Ground:** Bone `#F2E8D5` (warm off-white paper with grain)
- **Ink:** Espresso `#1C1814` (structural near-black)
- **Accents (one bold spot-colour per mark):** Terracotta `#B5482E` · Ochre `#D9923D` ·
  Cobalt `#2D4A8E` · Oxblood `#8A2B1F`
- **Print language:** flat spot colour, deliberate ink **misregistration**, visible **halftone** dots,
  registration crop-marks — a zine/silkscreen feel, not a tech gradient.

The eight span the range of **form** (not just metaphor): four V-lockups, one pure symbol, one type-only
wordmark, one app-icon reduction, and one dark dramatic anchor — with a calm curatorial mark to land the set.

## Concepts

### 01 · SMEAR — `marks-v2/vitrine-v2-01-smear.png`  · *primary V-lockup*
> *The ghost snaps sharp.*

- **Palette:** Bone `#F2E8D5` · Espresso `#1C1814` · Cobalt `#2D4A8E`
- **Type:** Heavy grotesque, tracked tight
- **Motif:** A thick wet ink brush-stroke dragged sideways that **snaps** at its leading edge into a razor-edged **V**, a halftone motion-blur trail chasing behind.
- **Rationale:** The apparition resolving into structure *mid-transit* — the single most literal picture of "noise becoming geometry." A verb, not a noun. The most flexible primary mark; the cobalt is the only cool note in an otherwise warm system, so it pops as the hero.

### 02 · TREMOR — `marks-v2/vitrine-v2-02-tremor.png`  · *structural contrast*
> *Reading tremors, mapping bedrock.*

- **Palette:** Bone `#F2E8D5` · Espresso `#1C1814` · Oxblood `#8A2B1F`
- **Type:** Industrial sans, wide caps
- **Motif:** A jagged seismograph line crashing down into a heavy **V drawn as stratified rock cross-section**, a puff of halftone dust at the impact.
- **Rationale:** Photogrammetry as a **geological event** — Vitrine reads the *tremors* of a shaky handheld camera to map the solid *bedrock* of the mesh. Bold, poster-graphic, and it visualises the exact thing capture-adaptive reconstruction does: turn motion into structure.

### 03 · BELL — `marks-v2/vitrine-v2-03-bell.png`  · *dark anchor*
> *Noise preserved under heavy glass.*

- **Palette:** Espresso `#1C1814` (ground) · Ochre `#D9923D` · Oxblood `#8A2B1F`
- **Type:** High-contrast editorial serif
- **Motif:** A brutalist **bell-jar** silhouette trapping a swirling warm-ink particle storm; the base cuts up into a V.
- **Rationale:** The dramatic dark-mode variant and the board's visual weight. It flips the vitrine cliché — the case is **heavy and dark**, but the *noise it preserves* is warm and alive: the archive of chaos, not the escape from it. For signage, title cards, dark UI.

### 04 · PLUCK — `marks-v2/vitrine-v2-04-pluck.png`  · *agentic / curator*
> *Plucking form from falling static.*

- **Palette:** Terracotta `#B5482E` · Espresso `#1C1814` · Ochre `#D9923D`
- **Type:** Precise monospace labels
- **Motif:** Heavy Swiss **tweezers** (an inverted V) gripping one solid primitive while a cascade of ochre halftone static falls away, discarded.
- **Rationale:** Vitrine's **agentic, diagnostic** intelligence made curatorial — the software actively *plucks* the good, game-ready asset out of the noise. Turns an algorithm into a gesture. Very ownable; nobody else in the category draws tweezers.

### 05 · SIEVE — `marks-v2/vitrine-v2-05-sieve.png`  · *pure symbol*
> *Chaos filtered, order revealed.*

- **Palette:** Bone `#F2E8D5` · Espresso `#1C1814` · Ochre `#D9923D` · Cobalt `#2D4A8E`
- **Type:** Neo-grotesque, structural
- **Motif:** A funnel catching irregular ochre blobs at the top and dropping a perfect **cobalt tessellated grid** out the bottom. No letterform.
- **Rationale:** The **capture-adaptive routing thesis** as a brutalist data-diagram: messy input → filter → structured output. The set's one pure symbol — works standalone as an app glyph or process icon, independent of the wordmark.

### 06 · MOLD — `marks-v2/vitrine-v2-06-mold.png`  · *app-icon*
> *Cracking open the pristine twin.*

- **Palette:** Terracotta `#B5482E` · Cobalt `#2D4A8E` · Bone `#F2E8D5`
- **Type:** Geometric sans, UI-legible
- **Motif:** A rough plaster **V split by a jagged crack**, the halves pulled apart to reveal a glowing smooth cobalt core.
- **Rationale:** The negative-cast metaphor — crack open the disposable, messy capture to extract the pristine, game-ready **twin** inside. Chunky and ultra-high-contrast: the mark that survives to 64×64 favicon / app-icon.

### 07 · OFFSET — `marks-v2/vitrine-v2-07-offset.png`  · *wordmark*
> *Two worlds, slightly off-register.*

- **Palette:** Bone `#F2E8D5` · Espresso `#1C1814` · Terracotta `#B5482E`
- **Type:** Heavy block grotesque
- **Motif:** `VITRINE` printed in **two misaligned ink layers** — a terracotta layer shifted under a stark espresso layer — with registration crop-marks.
- **Rationale:** The **uncanny doubling** of the digital twin, told through a print imperfection: the real and the virtual occupying the same space, slightly unhinged. Type-only, no symbol — the set's hero wordmark, and the clearest statement of the risograph register.

### 08 · SPECIMEN — `marks-v2/vitrine-v2-08-specimen.png`  · *curatorial close*
> *Pinned, tagged, preserved in ink.*

- **Palette:** Bone `#F2E8D5` · Espresso `#1C1814` · Ochre `#D9923D`
- **Type:** Inscriptional Roman caps
- **Motif:** A reconstructed object rendered as an antique **museum specimen plate** — fine ink line-engraving, a letterpress **catalogue tag** ("VITRINE No. 002"), a faint splat-dot **provenance aura**.
- **Rationale:** The warm, quiet counterweight that lands the set — "digital taxidermy" without the gore. It fuses **archival curation** with the point-cloud origin (the dots are the scan's provenance), and gives the brand its editorial, museum-catalogue voice. The calm last word after seven violent ones.

## GLM dialogue — how the ideas were generated

The concept space was fanned out and converged through a **genuine multi-turn conversation** with
**ZAI GLM**, not a single query.

- **Model / endpoint that worked:** `glm-5.2` via the OpenAI-compatible endpoint
  `POST https://api.z.ai/api/paas/v4/chat/completions` (`Authorization: Bearer $ZAI_API_KEY`).
  GLM-5.2 answered on the first try — it is a *reasoning* model (returns `reasoning_content` and
  spends tokens thinking), so calls used `max_tokens: 6000`. No fallback to glm-4.6/4.5 was needed.
- **Four turns** of real back-and-forth (seed → critique/redirect → converge → lock); highlights:

**Turn 1 — fan out.** I seeded the full ontology (splats, point clouds, capture/scanning, optics,
museum/vitrine, archival/memory, French connotations, emergence-from-noise, digital taxidermy) and
asked GLM *not* to give logos yet but to map the weird adjacencies. It returned 18 territories —
the keepers were **Digital Taxidermy**, **The Apparition**, **Agentic Tweezers**, **Atmosphere Under
Glass** (bell jar), **The Pulled Thread**, **The Negative Cast**, **Seismic Shift**.

**Turn 2 — critique & redirect.** I fenced off v1's territory (no glass cubes, refraction shards,
camera-iris apertures, cool cyber palettes; cut "Glass Slicing", "Caustic Etching", "Bloom to Facet")
and steered hard into a **different aesthetic register**: bolder/graphic, warmer/editorial,
more motion/energy, willing to be weird. GLM converted the ontology into 10 candidate marks —
**SMEAR, PLUCK, BELL, MOLD, OFFSET, TAXIDERMY/TREMOR, SIEVE, WIPE, TENSION, OFFSET**.

**Turn 3 — converge on a family.** I pushed back that too many were "a V made of X" and demanded
**range of form**: keep the killer V-lockups, but add a pure symbol, a wordmark-led idea, an
app-icon reduction, and a dark anchor. GLM locked 8 with palettes, type, and board roles — and
picked **TREMOR over TAXIDERMY** for graphic poster energy.

**Turn 4 — final lock.** I rejected GLM's proposed "PLINTH" (v1 already owned a plinth mark) and
proposed **SPECIMEN** (the museum specimen-plate metaphor) instead; GLM approved it and sharpened it
(the catalogue tag should carry real accession-number metadata, tying back to the pipeline's
diagnostic intelligence), then delivered the final 8 with taglines and a 4×2 board reading order:
*top row = transformation energy (smear, crash, trap, extract); bottom row = resolution into
structure (route, reveal, register, preserve); BELL anchors the dark weight, SPECIMEN lands quiet.*

GLM's own one-line summary of the system: *"If v1 was the cool, precise, thin-line glass cube, v2 is
the warm, violent, ink-stained hand that casts the mold — trading quiet archival restraint for the
messy, physical, editorial energy of a reconstruction in progress."*
