# Vitrine — Funding Landscape Findings (2025–2027)

_Compiled 2026-07-02. Scope: concrete, current funding opportunities for **Vitrine**
(University of Salford / MediaCityUK): capture-adaptive 3D Gaussian Splatting →
game-ready structured 3D scenes in Unreal Engine 5.8, on-prem hardened Docker, for
cultural-heritage digitisation and immersive/creative applications._

> **Alignment with prior positioning.** This builds on the submitted **AI KE CoP
> pilot bid (V3)** — _"AI Workflow Solutions towards the Efficient Preservation of
> Immersive and Intangible Cultural Heritage in Virtual Spaces with DreamLab AI"_
> (Roger McKinley, MediaCity Campus; Worktribe 5043317). That bid already names the
> **EU anchor** — Horizon Europe 2027 Cluster 2, **HORIZON-CL2-2027-01-HERITAGE-08
> (Safeguarding & transmission of intangible cultural heritage)** — with partners
> Rhizome (USA), Onassis ONX (USA/Greece), and proposed Waag FutureLab and V2 (NL).
> The landscape below keeps that anchor and adds the realistic UK stepping-stones
> and adjacent EU/UK routes to get there. See
> `docs/brief/ai-cop-bid-v3.md` and `docs/brief/ai-cop-funding-pitch-2026.md`.

## Horizon positioning narrative

**UK is back in, so bid direct.** UK entities are **associated to Horizon Europe**
for the 2024–2027 work programmes and receive **direct European Commission funding**
in Cluster 2 (Pillar II) — no need for the UKRI guarantee for associated calls. The
one caveat to check per-call: a small number of topics funded from pre-association
underspend can exclude UK funding, so read each call's eligibility conditions on the
Funding & Tenders Portal. (Refs below.)

**The anchor is real and well-timed.** The submitted CoP bid's EU target —
**HORIZON-CL2-2027-01-HERITAGE-08, "Safeguarding & transmission of intangible
cultural heritage"** — is confirmed in the published **Horizon Europe 2026-2027
Cluster 2 work programme** (~€3–4m per project, ~3 projects, under the "European
Cultural Heritage and the Cultural and Creative Industries" destination). ICH is the
right frame for Vitrine's distinctive claim: not scanning static objects, but
**capturing live, performed, time-based events** (Nested Cinema, Lightwaves, We
Invented the Weekend) into a navigable, game-ready 3D "low-resolution preservation
space." Provisional 2027 timing (open ~13 May, close ~23 Sep 2027) means the CoP
pilot is the sequencing-correct **de-risking / proof-of-principle** step.

**Don't wait for 2027 to get EU-visible.** Two earlier moves strengthen the
HERITAGE-08 bid: (1) the **2026** AI-in-CCI-practice topic
(**HORIZON-CL2-2026-01-HERITAGE-03**) is arguably an even cleaner fit for Vitrine's
"efficient AI workflow" pitch and lands a year sooner; (2) the **European
Collaborative Cloud for Cultural Heritage (ECCCH / ECHOES)** is the EU's flagship
digital-heritage infrastructure and explicitly wants **innovative tools for 3D and
high-value interactions with heritage objects** — Vitrine is a natural tool
contribution. The near-term ECHOES **cascading grant** (non-profit CHIs, deadline
**30 Jan 2026**) is a way to seed an ECCCH relationship via a heritage partner
before a full tool-development call in WP2026-27.

**Creative Europe is a weak direct route for the UK.** The UK is **not a
participating country**; third-country access to the **Culture strand** requires a
specific agreement the UK does not currently hold, and MEDIA is more restricted
still. Practically, Vitrine reaches Creative Europe only as a **non-funded partner**
inside an EU-led Culture cooperation project (leveraging Waag/V2/Onassis) — treat as
relationship-building, not a funding line.

**The UK ladder that gets you to the EU bid.** Sequence the domestic funders as
rungs: **MITIH** (immediate, local, Greater Manchester creative-tech micro-grants /
residency) → **Innovate UK Creative Catalyst** (DreamLab-led CR&D productisation) →
**CoSTAR** and **Creative Industries Clusters round two** (embed Vitrine as shared
real-time/games capability in a MediaCity-hosted cluster) and **AHRC / N-RICH**
(national digitisation-capability + the sovereignty/cyber/environmental frameworks
Vitrine's hardened on-prem Docker directly answers). **National Lottery Heritage
Grants** (rolling) fund cultural partners to run real digitisation case studies. Each
rung produces the case histories, partnerships and de-risking the CoP bid promised as
prerequisites for HERITAGE-08.

**Vitrine's four-way differentiator to lead with in every bid:** heritage
digitisation **+** immersive/games-engine delivery (UE5.8, Nanite) **+**
**sovereign / edge / on-prem** AI (hardened Docker, "own your data" — a strong hook
for N-RICH cybersecurity, GLAM data-governance, and EU digital-sovereignty framing)
**+** real games-industry asset workflow (FBX game assets, not archival-only USD).

## Uncertain / deadline-sensitive — verify before relying on

- **Horizon CL2 dates are provisional.** Open ~12 May 2026 / ~13 May 2027, deadline
  ~23 Sep in each year, per the published WP2026-2027 — confirm exact dates and
  final budgets on the **EU Funding & Tenders Portal** nearer the time.
- **ECHOES cascading 2nd call closes 30 Jan 2026** and is **non-profit CHIs only** —
  UoS/DreamLab cannot lead; needs a heritage-institution partner. Move now if wanted.
- **ECCCH innovative-use-cases (WP2025) call** already closed (16 Sep 2025); the next
  **tool-development** ECCCH call sits in WP2026-27 — not yet dated. Track it.
- **Innovate UK Creative Catalyst** last round closed 29 Jan 2025; reopening not yet
  confirmed — monitor the Innovation Funding Service.
- **CreativeXR** current cohort/opening status not confirmed for 2026 — verify with
  Digital Catapult before pitching.
- **MITIH Fund 2025-26 Round 3** is live but round-based — check the current window.
- **Creative Europe** direct UK funding: **not available** without a specific
  UK–EU agreement; partner-only route.
